# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Academic admissions panels, research supervisors, technical recruiters and engineering leaders evaluating Sergio Antunez Rios for applied AI, computer vision, biomedical engineering and interdisciplinary research opportunities. They need to understand his profile, verify evidence and identify relevant projects quickly in either Spanish or English.

## Product Purpose

Present a credible bilingual record of applied research and enterprise product work. The portfolio should make Sergio discoverable for relevant academic and technical opportunities while showing measurable outcomes, documented limitations and public-safe evidence without exposing confidential company architecture or unsupported claims.

## Brand Personality

Academic, rigorous and physically grounded. The voice is confident but careful: specific evidence leads, technical language is explained and uncertainty is labelled rather than hidden.

## Anti-references

- Generic developer portfolios built around tool logos, skill percentages or decorative terminal interfaces.
- Dark, futuristic AI aesthetics that obscure the engineering evidence.
- Unverified superlatives, inflated financial impact or editorial-status claims.
- Public disclosure of internal project names, sensitive architecture, providers or operational details.
- Dense academic pages that require a specialist to understand the practical contribution.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the problem, contribution and observable result.
2. Make every important claim traceable to evidence or clearly label it as projected.
3. Explain interdisciplinary work in language legible to both researchers and technical leaders.
4. Keep Spanish and English narratives equivalent, not mechanically translated.
5. Protect confidential work while preserving enough specificity to demonstrate value.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain semantic landmarks and heading order, keyboard-visible focus, descriptive alternative text, 44 px touch targets, sufficient contrast, responsive text and reduced-motion support. Language and alternate-page metadata must remain accurate for Spanish and English readers.
